Mushroom Risotto

This is Mushroom Risotto, not to be confused with "Mushroom Risotto."

Ah yes, mushroom risotto. An old classic of Italy (I think). Mushroom Risotto is a dish known to have originated from the great man known as Otto, known for having exceptional "rizz." Thus the name risotto! This is a dish that is delicious and easy to master, so long as you have two working arms. Let's get started with how to make it!

Ingredients:

  • Portobello Mushrooms
  • Arbolio Rice
  • Onions/Shallots
  • Garlic
  • Thyme
  • Salt
  • Peppah
  • Oil
  • Vinegar

Steps:

  1. Acquire items, cut mushrooms into bitesized pieces, dice the onions, and mince the garlic.
  2. Oh yeah also don't forget to wash the rice.
  3. Put in the onions and garlic into the pan, and continuously stir for around 2 minutes
  4. Add in the mushrooms, and put in salt, pepper, and thyme till your heart's content.
  5. Keep stirring, then add in the washed rice along with a cup of water.
  6. While simultaneously making sure nothing sticks to the pan, stir around until the rice absorbs the water, when this happens, add in 1/2 cup of water, stir, and repeat.
  7. You should do the above step around 4-5 times
  8. Serve and enjoy!
